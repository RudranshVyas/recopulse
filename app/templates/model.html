Those numbers look better than they are.

The purchase predictor is mostly doing one thing: checking whether someone put something in their cart. In this data, nobody who skipped the cart ever bought anything — not once in nearly 11,000 visits. That's an unusually clean pattern, and the predictor leans on it heavily.

To show how much it leans, we rebuilt the whole thing with cart activity hidden from it. Its ability to spot buyers falls apart:

Most shoppers touch only a handful of products, so popular items are well understood and rare ones are not. Suggestions drawn from the quiet corners of the catalog rest on very few examples and deserve less trust than the confident-looking ranking implies.

About this data

RecoPulse runs on a simulated store — 10,000 invented shoppers and a year of invented browsing, not real customers. The behaviour patterns are realistic enough to build and test against, but a few are cleaner than real life ever is (the cart rule above being the obvious one). Real shoppers are messier, and the numbers on this page would be lower against them.
