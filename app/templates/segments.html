Why three groups and not two?

Splitting the customers two ways scores slightly better on paper, but it only tells you "some people shop and some don't" — which nobody needs software to work out. It also hides the quiet majority who sign up and never come back.

Three groups is the next best split, and it's the one you can act on. It separates out the Heavy Browsers: 2,292 people who look at plenty of products and almost never buy. That's the group worth a nudge — and you can't see them at all in a two-way split.
